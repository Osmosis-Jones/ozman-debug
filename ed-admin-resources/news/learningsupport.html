Learning Support: Every Student is Precious

Vivian suffers from attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).

In many ways, she seems like an ordinary 15-year-old student. And of course, every ordinary is somewhat unique, right? She is sharp, clever, and sometimes funny. She loves history, visual art, and alternative rock and plays in her school’s football team. She has just started learning the Hang and is getting better by the day. She is surrounded by a loving family and is a prominent member of a like-minded social group of teens.

But, there is a troubling side to this unique-ordinary life. Her teachers noticed she sometimes fixates on a problem for hours, unable to let go, and some days she simply can’t bring herself around to focus on anything.

What at first seemed like a common teenage turbulence of hormones, was exacerbated over time. Teachers started chastising her for her “odd behaviour”. And even though she knows she has ADHD, the thought of explaining her condition every time to every teacher, principal, and staff in front of her schoolmates is unbearable.

There are many students like Vivian in every educational institution. And I'm not talking about people with ADHD only, but all students with severe conditions that require special care. Hearing impairment, mobility issues, chronic disorders, and more.
